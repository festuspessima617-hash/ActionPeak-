Awareness & education

Learning is the first step toward lasting protection.

ActionPeak delivers clear, caring messages that help communities recognize abuse and prevent harm.

Why awareness matters

Understanding gender-based violence helps survivors, allies, and community leaders respond with compassion and confidence. We focus on prevention, support, and breaking silence.

ActionPeak builds awareness in three key areas: recognizing signs of abuse, learning safe reporting steps, and creating strong networks of community care. Each session combines practical guidance with stories and local solutions.

What is GBV?

Gender-based violence includes any harmful action directed at a person because of their gender or gender expression. It can be physical, emotional, economic, or sexual. In Sierra Leone, GBV affects women, girls, and vulnerable populations disproportionately, impacting their health, education, and ability to participate fully in society.

Community impact

GBV affects health, education, trust, and the ability to thrive. Awareness is key to healing and prevention, not only for survivors but for families and leaders. When violence remains hidden, it spreads fear and limits opportunities for entire communities to grow and prosper.

How to prevent it

Speaking up, supporting survivors, and educating families and schools are powerful ways to stop abuse. Prevention also means changing harmful attitudes and building safe spaces. This includes teaching boys and men about respect, teaching girls their rights, and creating accountability for harmful behavior.

Community champions

We train local leaders, teachers, and young people to recognize unsafe situations and to respond with care and confidence. These champions become trusted voices in their communities, helping families understand the signs of abuse and the importance of intervention.

Safe reporting

Survivors need practical steps and confidentiality. Our workshops explain how to report safely and what happens after a report is made. We help survivors understand their rights and the support services available to them, from counseling to legal aid.

Supporting survivors

Survivor support includes medical care, counseling, legal assistance, and community follow-up so no one feels alone. We believe in a holistic approach that recognizes the unique needs of each survivor and respects their agency in healing.

Understanding the signs of abuse

Recognizing signs of abuse is the first step to intervention. Here are common warning signs in relationships and families:

  • Physical abuse: Unexplained injuries, bruises, burns, or frequent "accidents."
  • Emotional abuse: Constant criticism, humiliation, isolation from family and friends, or control over daily activities.
  • Economic abuse: Control over money, preventing work, or stealing earnings.
  • Sexual abuse: Unwanted sexual contact, harassment, or coercion into sexual acts.
  • Behavioral changes: Sudden withdrawal, anxiety, depression, or fear around specific people.

Steps to take if you suspect abuse

  1. Listen without judgment. If someone discloses abuse, believe them and thank them for their courage.
  2. Ensure safety first. Help the person get to a safe place if they are in immediate danger.
  3. Provide information. Share resources like our hotline, local shelters, counseling services, and legal aid.
  4. Support reporting. Accompany them to report to police or seek medical care if they choose to do so.
  5. Follow up with care. Check in regularly and help connect them with ongoing support services.
  6. Maintain confidentiality. Respect the survivor's privacy and safety throughout the process.

Frequently asked questions about GBV awareness

What should I do if a family member is experiencing abuse?

First, listen and believe them. Offer emotional support and help them connect with resources like our hotline, medical services, or counseling. Never blame the survivor. Work with them to create a safety plan and respect their decisions about how to move forward. If there is immediate danger, call emergency services.

How can I help prevent GBV in my community?

Engage in awareness activities, speak openly about the harms of violence, challenge harmful stereotypes, support survivors, and report suspected abuse to authorities or organizations like ActionPeak. Educate young people about consent and healthy relationships, and work with community leaders to establish safe reporting mechanisms.

Is there confidentiality when reporting abuse?

Yes. ActionPeak takes confidentiality seriously. Reports are handled with discretion and shared only with those who need to know to provide support and follow up. Survivors maintain the right to decide what information is shared and with whom.
